Hack with us each month!

Where and when

The next eConvenor hack day is Saturday April 11th, 2015 in Melbourne, Australia.

From 10:00am to 5:30pm we work on eConvenor at 8 Studley Street, Abbotsford. Thanks to Common Code for making their space available for us to use.

From 6:00pm we relax and dine at Loving Hut, 10/242 Victoria St, Richmond.

Accessibility

The space has no front step, a slight ramp (15cms), a wide front doorway, and accessible toilets. Toilets have gendered signs, we treat them as genderless. The walkway on the street is narrow. There is parking out the front (no ramp), and public transport nearby.

Loving Hut in Richmond has a 15cm-20cm front step, standard-width door and average space between tables. Toilet doors are very narrow.

What to bring

All you need to bring is your computer. Having a Github account would also be handy.

We'll supply lunch and snacks throughout the day. It will help us cater if you let us know you're coming.

Code of conduct

We are committed to ending oppression, and a tiny part of that is making our space safer for all.

Please read our code of conduct. Everyone is expected to abide by it.

There's something for everyone to do

Back end (Django/Python) tasks

  • Allow a user to upload and use their group's logo (Ticket #41)
  • Enable the creation of reusable agenda templates (Ticket #51)
  • Enable a message to be added to agenda/minutes covering emails (Ticket #52)
  • Allow tags to be added to tasks (Ticket #7)
  • Configuring Sphinx for documentation generation

Front end (HTML/CSS/JS) tasks

  • Count words in some fields, not characters (Ticket #44)
  • Add an HTML version of password reset email (Ticket #49)
  • At least one recipient must be selected for document distribution (Ticket #12)

SEO

  • Improve eConvenor's seach engine rankings

Design

  • Design new graphic elements for the landing site

Non-technical

  • We need process- and systems-oriented people to test features, review eConvenor's workflow, and devise processes for new features. You don't need special IT skills to do this.
