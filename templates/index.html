Take the hard work out of organising your group.

A free tool for the progressive activist community.

Clear, well-organised agendas

A good agenda is the foundation for a good meeting. eConvenor makes compiling and distributing an excellent agenda easy, whether you're a veteran or you've never made one before.

  • Adds lists of outstanding tasks
  • Produces a neat PDF document
  • Emails it straight to your group
  • Includes a meeting summary in the email
  • Designed to encourage productive meetings
  • Calculates the meeting end time
  • Prompts for all the important information

Your group's best-ever minutes

Meeting minutes are so important, yet often overlooked or rushed. eConvenor makes it quick, easy and surprisingly painless to produce the best minutes your group has ever had.

  • Agendas automatically create minutes templates
  • Templates have special sections for tasks and decisions
  • eConvenor turns minutes into a PDF document and emails them out
  • Tasks assigned are summarised in the PDF and the email body
  • eConvenor's minutes design is based on 30 years of experience

Automatic task coordination

Do you wish that someone else could monitor the tasks assigned within your group and remind people when they need to be done? eConvenor will do that for you.

  • Collects and tracks tasks assigned in meetings
  • Emails reminders to people when their tasks are nearly due, and again if they become overdue
  • Participants can manage their own tasks, without logging in.
  • Tasks can be added any time
  • Lists of outstanding, overdue and completed tasks appear in agendas and minutes
  • eConvenor handles the often-awkward job of reminding people about their tasks for you

Built by activists, for activists.

eConvenor is a free service to help activist groups everywhere be more effective.

Who is eConvenor for?

eConvenor is for grassroots campaigns, non-government organisations (NGOs), progressive service providers and activist groups. Your group must have a socially-just, progressive focus.

eConvenor is for groups like these

    • A rights collective in the Philippines

    • which is trying to improve wheelchair access to public buildings in their local area. They have 12 activists and no budget.

    • A family violence crisis service in the UK

    • which is run by 7 staff, who help 300 women every year. They have a volunteer board of directors.

    • An anti-fracking campaign in Poland

    • which has a national committee, local sub-committees and 200 campaigners. Their budget stretches to printing posters and paying one part-time staff member.

It's not for everyone though

We built eConvenor to make the world a better place.

    • You must not use eConvenor if your group:

    •   is conservative or oppressive

    •   operates for profit

    •   is a government agency

    •   aims to spread religion.

Sometimes the best person for the job is a computer.

eConvenor does its work on time, has high standards and never forgets anything.

eConvenor is free for most groups

We aim to make the eConvenor service available as widely as we can. This means eConvenor is free for almost everyone, and we ask better funded groups to contribute financially.

From each according to their means

    • Most groups

    • Does your group have no budget or a tiny budget? eConvenor was built for you! You can use eConvenor completely free. No obligation, no worries.

    • Groups which can afford to contribute

    • Groups who feel that they can afford to make a donation in return for using eConvenor should do so once each year.

    • Political parties and trade unions

    • Political parties and trade unions in high income countries who are progressive and committed to social justice can use eConvenor for a fee.

We rely on your honesty. We hope you will apply your ethics and contribute if you should. Donations help eConvenor improve.

Help build eConvenor

    • Got coding or web design skills?

    • Help us build eConvenor into a powerful tool which helps activist groups around the world. We're looking for people with skills in coding (Python/Django), web development (HTML/CSS/JS), web application security, graphic design and writing/communications.

    • No specialist skills but lots of initiative?

    • There are always non-technical tasks which need doing, and the only pre-requisite for working on them is an ability to take some initiative and solve problems. If you're the right sort of person, we'll find ways for you to work on eConvenor with us no matter what your skills are.

    • Got 5 minutes to report a bug?

    • If eConvenor does something unexpected while you're using it, take a few minutes to tell us! We'll fix the problem and eConvenor will be a better service for everyone as a result.
